Why Investors Own Gold

Gold remains a cornerstone of sophisticated portfolios for well-established reasons. It has historically preserved purchasing power during periods of currency debasement and inflation. Its low correlation with equities and bonds provides genuine diversification when it matters most. And it serves as portfolio insurance against tail risks: geopolitical instability, banking crises, and systemic shocks.

Today, interest in gold is rising again. Central banks have become net buyers at multi-decade highs. Sovereign debt levels continue to climb. And allocators increasingly seek hard assets as a hedge against an uncertain monetary environment.

The case for gold is clear. The question is how to own it most effectively.

The Limitations of Traditional Gold

Gold's value proposition is compelling. But the infrastructure through which it has historically been accessed creates meaningful friction.

  • No yield. Unlike bonds, equities, or real estate, traditional gold generates no income. It sits inert, providing protection but no return on capital, a significant opportunity cost for allocators managing to benchmarks.
  • Custody drag. Storage, insurance, and administration typically cost 0.25-0.50% annually. These costs compound over time and never stop.
  • Settlement friction. Traditional gold markets operate on T+2 or longer settlement. Large transactions require coordination between custodians and counterparties, limiting portfolio flexibility.

These limitations don't diminish gold's value. They highlight the opportunity for infrastructure improvement.

What Is Tokenized Gold?

Tokenized gold is physical gold represented as digital tokens on secure blockchain infrastructure. We utilize PAXG (Pax Gold), issued by Paxos Trust Company, LLC, a New York limited purpose trust company regulated by the New York State Department of Financial Services. Each PAXG token corresponds to exactly one fine troy ounce of allocated London Good Delivery gold held in LBMA-accredited vaults in London.

How It Works

PAXG can be purchased with USD or unallocated gold, which is then converted into allocated gold bars through an LBMA clearer. Tokens are minted in relation to those specific bars. At any time, PAXG holders can see the serial number, value, and physical characteristics of their vaulted gold. Independent auditors verify that total PAXG tokens outstanding match the underlying gold assets.

Independent Verification

KPMG LLP conducts independent accountants' examinations of Paxos's PAXG Redemption Assets Report. Their examination verifies that total redemption assets (London Good Delivery gold held in LBMA-approved vaults) are equal to or greater than the total PAXG redeemable tokens outstanding. This provides institutional-grade assurance of full backing.
